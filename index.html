+ 1. Demographic Shifts: The Over-60s Are a Growing Resource
  • 19% of New Zealanders are 60+ now; 25% by 2043 (Stats NZ).
  • Wellington has the highest percentage of degree holders; tertiary attainment in over-60s is rising.
  • Labour force participation for 65+ is up to 25% and growing (MBIE 2022).
+ 2. Innovation, Productivity & Business Value
  • Mixed-age teams produce more patents and higher-impact innovations (Harvard Business Review).
  • People aged 55-64 are more likely to start a business than those aged 20-34 (NZ/US data).
  • Older entrepreneurs have higher business survival rates (OECD).
+ 3. The Over-60s & Digital Capability
  • 72% of Kiwis aged 65+ use the internet daily (Stats NZ 2022).
  • Many build websites, run online stores, and manage business social media (AUT 2022).
  • Participation in coding bootcamps among 55+ is up 300% since 2017 (NZTech 2023).
+ 4. Benefits to Wellington’s Business Community
  • Experience brings judgement and stability—critical for risk management and growth.
  • Over-60s make ideal mentors and knowledge-transfer agents, reducing brain drain.
  • Mixed-age teams outperform homogenous ones in creative output (Deloitte 2019).
  • Deep local and international business networks.
+ 5. Addressing Stereotypes & Bias
  • The idea that over-60s are digital novices is outdated.
  • Average age of a successful tech founder is 45 (Harvard 2018).
  • Under-utilising experienced workers costs the economy billions (NZIER 2021).
+ 6. Recommendations
  • Reframe the narrative: “Main Branch” as core contributors.
  • Recruit older professionals for mentorship, leadership, and technical roles.
  • Promote intergenerational teams and fund cross-age projects.
  • Provide upskilling and digital training—tailored for the experienced.
  • Publicise Wellington-based 60+ innovation stories.
+ Conclusion

Wellington can’t afford to sideline a quarter of its potential workforce. The over-60s are ready and able to build, mentor, innovate, and stabilise the city’s business ecosystem. Let’s shift from a narrative of “step aside” to “step up”—the Main Branch is where the real work gets done.
